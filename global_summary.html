Summary of Pension System in Selected Jurisdictions

Hong Kong

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 20 years
  • Mandatory Provident Fund (private DC scheme) / Occupational Retirement Schemes Ordinance (private DB scheme)
    • Mandatory pension scheme for employees and self-employed aged 18-64
  • Replacement rate: 40.0% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: 1.22% GDP in 2022 (excluding CSSA and disability allowance)

China

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 24 years
  • Public Pension Fund (public DB scheme)
    • 2 tiers: urban-rural basic pension and mandatory urban employee pension
    • Mandatory for all employees, excluding self-employed
    • Contribution rate differs across provinces
    • Only 1.5 billion of the population is insured
  • Replacement rate: 90.8% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Voluntary occupational pension (enterprise annuity) only covers 3% workforce
  • Individual Retirement Account (first private pension in China) was introduced in 2022

Singapore

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 21 years (retirement age is extending to 67)
  • Central Provident Fund (private DC scheme)
    • Mandatory to employees of any age
    • Contribution rate at 12.5-37% depends on age
    • Contributions earn a basic interest rate of 2.5-6%
  • Replacement rate: 61.6% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: less than 1% GDP

Japan

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 19 years
  • National basic pension (public DB scheme)
    • Mandatory for residents (non-salary worker) aged 20-59
    • Flat-rate contribution of JPY16,520
  • Employees' Pension Insurance (public DB scheme)
    • A pay-as-you-go plan for salary workers
    • Mandatory contribution rate at 18.3%, shared by employers and employees
  • Replacement rate: 38.7% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: 9.6% GDP, covers for 18% of gross average earnings

Korea

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 22 years
  • National Pension Service (public DB scheme)
    • Mandatory to all residents ages 18-59, only 60% of them actually contribute
    • Contribution rate at 9%, exemption granted for qualified individuals
  • Replacement rate: 35.4% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: 3.6% GDP, covers only 8% gross average earnings
  • Voluntary corporate pension is often paid in form of unfunded lump-sum severance payment

The US

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 19 years
  • * Occupation pension plans are mandatory in only 14 states
  • Heavily rely on private pensions, more than 1000 plans are available in the market
  • About 48% private sector employees are not covered by employer-sponsored pension
  • Replacement rate: 50.5% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: 7.5% GDP, covers 15.6% gross average earnings

Canada

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 18 years
  • Canadian Pension Plan (public DB scheme)
    • Mandatory for all working Canadians ages 18-70
    • Contribution rate at 11.9%, shared equally by employees and employers
  • Replacement rate: 46.4% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: 5.27% GDP, covers 19.2% gross average earnings

Iceland (#1)

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 16 years
  • Mandatory Pension Fund (private DB-DC hybrid scheme)
    • Covers all workers aged 16-70
    • Contribution rate at 15.5%: 4% from employee, 11.5% from employer
  • Replacement rate: 59.7% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public spending on pillar 0: 2.86% GDP which covers 51% gross average earnings
  • 45.2% working population participate in voluntary personal pension
  • Employers match contribution on 2-4% additional voluntary contribution

The Netherlands (#2)

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 14 years
  • AOW (non-contributory basic pension, tax-funded)
  • Private occupational pension scheme available on collective industry agreement, most of them are mandatory
  • Replacement rate: 85.3% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Pension reform in progress, pension funds are required to switch from DB to DC plan no later than 2028 (currently vast majority are DB plans)

Denmark (#3)

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 13 years
  • ATP Livslang Pension (public DC scheme)
    • Mandatory to employees ages 16 or above, voluntary participation for self-employed
    • Contribution made by employer, amount varies by working hours
  • Private occupational DC pension scheme is mandatory, benefits based on collective industry agreement
  • Replacement rate: 84% for people earning average wage before retirement
  • Public expenditure on pillar 0: 8.1% GDP, covers 37% gross average earnings

Germany

  • Life expectancy at retirement: 15 years (retirement age will reach 67 by 2031)
  • RV (public DB scheme)
    • Mandatory to all people working in Germany
    • Contribution rate at 18.6%
  • Voluntary company pension covers around 60% population
  • Pillar 0 basic pension was introduced in 2021, covering 19.3% gross average earning
