Singapore Central Provident Fund

The Central Provident Fund managed by CPF Board is a key pillar of Singapore's social security system that helps Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents6 set aside funds to meet the three basic needs in retirement:

  1. A fully paid-up home
  2. Insurance and savings for healthcare
  3. A steady stream of lifelong retirement income

A comprehensive saving system with 4 accounts serving different needs:

  • Ordinary Account - for housing, education, insurance, and investment
    • Base interest: 2.5%
    • Individuals can invest their OA savings under CPFIS-OA after setting aside $20,000 (HKD 118,250) in the OA, up to 35% and 10% of your investible savings can be put in stocks and gold respectively
  • Special Account - for old age and investment in retirement-related financial products
    • Base interest: 4%
    • Employer matching contribution of $0.85 for every $1 contribution by employee
    • Individuals can invest their SA savings under CPFIS-SA after setting aside $40,000
  • MediSave Account - for hospitalisation expenses and approved medical insurance
    • Base interest: 4%
    • Can be used to pay the premium of mandatory healthcare insurance (Medishield Life)
    • Can be used for dependants' hospitalisation, day surgery, and certain outpatient expenses
  • Retirement Account - for monthly retirement payout
    • Base interest: 4%
    • Activated at age 55, individuals could withdraw CPF savings freely after setting aside the applicable retirement sum in RA for purchasing lifelong annuity (e.g. CPF LIFE)
    • Government will match every dollar of cash top-ups for people aged 55-70 who have yet to meet the current Basic Retirement Sum (BRS) up to a maximum of $600 (HKD 3,550) a year

Contribution

Participation is mandatory to employees of any age. Self-employed are only required to contribute to MediSave Account.
Mandatory contribution ranges from 12.5% to 37% and are allocated to different accounts. Both rates are depending on contributor's age. The split of contribution by employers and employees also varies with age.
Annual salary ceiling is at $102,000 (HKD 603,070); monthly ordinary wage 7 will be raised from $6000 (HKD 35,470) to $8000 (HKD 47,300) by 20268.
Tax-relief of up to $16,000 in each calendar year for cash top-ups (voluntary contribution) for own or immediate family members

Investment and Return

CPF savings (that are not used for individual investments) managed by CPF Board are invested in Special Singapore Government Securities (non-tradable bonds issued specifically for CPF savings) which are guaranteed by the Government to pay interest, the rate is reviewed quarterly.
The proceeds from SSGS issuance are invested by the government via MAS and GIC.
Investment is not linked to employment; employees have full control over their money, which is refer to as "full portability" [see Investment options under CPF Investment Scheme (CPFIS)]

  • Avoid holding of multiple accounts due to job change
  • Employers have no say on how employees manage their contributions

Payout

The amount saved in RA will be used to purchase national longevity insurance annuity scheme - CPF LIFE, which citizens and PRs with more than $60,000 in their RA are automatically enrolled. Opting out is possible if certain conditions are met.
Payout starts at age 65 the earliest. Individuals can choose to receive increasing, fixed, or decreasing monthly payout.

Notes

6. Only Singaporeans and permanent residents are eligible for CPF, others may consider the Supplementary Retirement Scheme (SRS) of which participation is voluntary

7. Equivalent to monthly relevant income in MPF

8. CPF Contribution Changes from 1 September 2023 and 1 January 2024
